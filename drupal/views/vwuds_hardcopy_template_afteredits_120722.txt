ANNUAL REPORT OF WATER WITHDRAWALS: January 1, 2016 to December 31, 2016
Enclosed are the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality\'s Annual Report of Water Withdrawals forms for reporting all water withdrawals from surface and/or groundwater sources during the period January 1, 2016 through December 31, 2016. Water withdrawal reporting is required under State Water Control Board (SWCB) Water Withdrawal Reporting Regulation (9 VAC 25-200-10), et seq. The purpose of withdrawal reporting is to enable appropriate planning for the State\'s future water needs through the collection of accurate information. A copy of the regulation is available on this website: http://www.deq.virginia.gov/Programs/Water/WaterSupplyWaterQuantity/WaterSupplyPlanning/AnnualWaterWithdrawalReporting.aspx Water Withdrawal Reporting may be submitted to DEQ either in a hardcopy format or online.
Reporting Requirements:
Withdrawals in Virginia for crop production (including nurseries and sod farms) must be reported by anyone whose withdrawal exceeds one million gallons in any single month. Withdrawals in Virginia for ALL other purposes (including, but not limited to livestock production, mining operations, public water supplies, manufacturing, power production, and golf courses) must be reported by anyone whose average daily withdrawal during any single month exceeds 10,000 gallons per day. The regulation requires that reporting information include the user’s name, address, sources and locations of the withdrawal, the cumulative volume of water withdrawn during each month of the calendar year, the maximum day withdrawal and the month in which it occurred, and the method of measurement used to calculate the withdrawal. Water users exempted from the regulation are encouraged to voluntarily report their water withdrawals. 
If you report groundwater withdrawals to DEQ under a groundwater withdrawal permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. You do not need to duplicate reporting for permitted groundwater withdrawals under this regulation. Your monthly information, submitted to your regional permit writer, is imported into the water withdrawal reporting database. However, surface water withdrawals that also occur at permitted groundwater withdrawal sites must be reported, either as part of your permitted groundwater withdrawal reporting or through this reporting process. If you report surface water withdrawals to DEQ under a Virginia Water Protection permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. In addition to this reporting requirement, you must also report under this regulation as instructed below.

Reporting Instructions: 
•	If you did not withdraw from one or more measuring points in 2016, but expect you may in future years, simply input zeros on the form in the appropriate location and note “no withdrawal this year” in the "Source Status" field.

Assistance in calculating your withdrawal volumes estimates is provided at the following website: http://www.deq.virginia.gov/Programs/Water/WaterSupplyWaterQuantity/WaterSupplyPlanning/AnnualWaterWithdrawalReporting.aspx. Description of Fields on Reporting Form:
ACTION TYPE - Identifies the appropriate action – write in or select one of the following from the drop down box: Withdrawal, Delivery, Release, Return, System Delivery, or System Release. If using online reporting, this field defaults to “Withdrawal” and you must make the appropriate selection from the drop down box. “Withdrawal” refers to any withdrawal of raw water. “Release” (reported by a wholesale waterworks) refers to the amount of finished or raw water sold by and released from a wholesale waterworks TO a recipient waterworks. The volume of water reported as released is usually based on a meter reading at the wholesale waterworks.A wholesale waterworks is a a waterworks that treats source water as necessary to produce finished water and then delivers some or all of that finished water to another waterworks. A consecutive waterworks is a waterworks that has no water production or source facility of its own and that obtains all of its water from another permitted waterworks or receives some or all of its finished water from one or more wholesale waterworks. “Delivery” (reported by the recipient) refers to the amount of finished or raw water purchased by and delivered to a recipient waterworks FROM a wholesale waterworks. The volume of water reported as delivered is usually based on a meter reading by the receiving waterworks. “In-System Release” refers to the water released from a water plant TO a service area within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks. The volume of water reported as an in-system release is usually based on a meter reading at the water plant. “In-System Delivery” refers to the water delivered to a service area FROM a raw water source or a water plant within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks.  The volume of water reported as an in-system delivery is usually based on a meter reading at the service area connection. “Return Flow” refers to surface water or groundwater withdrawals that are returned (discharged) to the same waterbody.  Return flows are essentially non-consumptive.

Examples may include dates of malfunctioning water meters, dates of source start up or abandonment, explanations for sharp changes in water withdrawal amounts due to temporary or permanent process shut downs or system changes, measurements reflecting clustered wells, etc.

INTAKE LOCATION – Write in or select the county, city, or town where the measuring point (source withdrawal) is located. 
LATITUDE AND LONGITUDE – Indicate the earth coordinates of the withdrawal intake location (please enter in decimal degrees).
MAXIMUM DAY withdrawal, MONTH MAX DAY occurred - Indicate the maximum withdrawal within a single day in million gallons (MG) and write in or select the month during which the maximum day occurred. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000.
MEASURING METHOD and SOURCE/CUSTOMER METERING- Select the method used to determine water withdrawal amounts, “Metered” or “Estimated.” If your withdrawals are metered, check "Source" if the meter is at the source; check "Customer" if it is metered anywhere other than at the source. If you estimate your withdrawal amounts, please describe the method used (e.g., pump time curves, etc.). 
SIC reference - Verify that we have you classified under the appropriate Standard Industrial Classification (SIC).  SIC is a system used by the United States Government for classifying industries. 
SOURCE STATUS - By default, this entry is set to "In Active Use" online, indicating that you did in fact withdraw water from this measuring point during the previous year. If there was no withdrawal or transfer of water at this measuring point during the previous year, but there is a plan to continue to use this measuring point in the future, then "No Withdrawal This Year" should be marked. If the withdrawal point or transfer is permanently abandoned, then "Permanently Abandoned" should be marked and the year the measuring point was removed from operation indicated by the reporter. Reporters are asked to contact the assigned DEQ planner for "permanently abandoned" confirmation. 
SOURCE TYPE – Write in or select the appropriate classification for the measuring point: Surface Water, Groundwater, or Transferred Water. Transferred Water is water that is withdrawn by a waterworks owner or operator and then transferred to another waterworks owner or operator. It is water bought or sold and is not a direct withdrawal from a source.
SUB-TYPE – Write in or select the raw water source sub-type as a Reservoir, Stream, Well, Spring, as appropriate.
WATER SOURCE/WELL NAME – Write in the commonly used name of the raw water source for the withdrawal, such as the “James River,” “Farm Pond,” or “Well #1.”
WATER WITHDRAWAL AMOUNT or BULK TRANSFERS - Report the withdrawal or transfer volumes in MG for JANUARY through DECEMBER. Enclosed are the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality\'s Annual Report of Water Withdrawals forms for reporting all water withdrawals from surface water and/or groundwater sources during the period January 1, 2018 through December 31, 2018. Water withdrawal reporting is required under State Water Control Board (SWCB) Water Withdrawal Reporting Regulation (9 VAC 25-200-10), et seq. The purpose of withdrawal reporting is to enable appropriate planning for the State\'s future water needs through the collection of accurate information. A copy of the regulation is available on this website: <a href="url">http://www.deq.virginia.gov/Programs/Water/WaterSupplyWaterQuantity/WaterSupplyPlanning/AnnualWaterWithdrawalReporting.aspx</a> Water Withdrawal Reporting may be submitted to DEQ either in a hardcopy format or online.

<b>Reporting Requirements:</b>

Withdrawals in Virginia for agricultural crop production (including nurseries and sod farms) must be reported by anyone whose withdrawal exceeds one million gallons in any single month.  Withdrawals in Virginia for ALL other purposes (including, but not limited to livestock production, mining operations, public water supplies, manufacturing, power production, and golf courses) must be reported by anyone whose average daily withdrawal during any single month exceeds 10,000 gallons per day.  The regulation requires that reporting information include the user’s name, address, sources and locations of the withdrawal, the cumulative volume of water withdrawn during each month of the calendar year, the maximum day withdrawal and the month in which it occurred, and the method of measurement used to calculate the withdrawal.  Water users exempted from the regulation may voluntarily report their water withdrawals. 

If you report groundwater withdrawals to DEQ under a groundwater withdrawal permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements.  You do not need to duplicate reporting for permitted groundwater withdrawals under this regulation.  DEQ imports the monthly information submitted to your regional permit writer into the water withdrawal reporting database. However, you must report the surface water withdrawals that also occur at permitted groundwater withdrawal sites, either as part of your permitted groundwater withdrawal reporting or through this reporting process.  If you report surface water withdrawals to DEQ under a Virginia Water Protection (VWP) permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. In addition to the VWP reporting requirement, you must also report under this regulation as instructed below.

<b>Description of Fields on Reporting Form:</b>
<ul>
<li><b>Action Type</b> - Identifies the appropriate action for transfers–circle one of the following: Release, Delivery. “Release” (reported by a wholesale waterworks) refers to the amount of finished or raw water sold by and released from a wholesale waterworks TO a recipient waterworks. The volume of water reported as released is usually based on a meter reading at the wholesale waterworks. A wholesale waterworks is a waterworks that treats source water as necessary to produce finished water and then delivers some or all of that finished water to another waterworks. A consecutive waterworks is a waterworks that has no water production or source facility of its own and that obtains all of its water from another permitted waterworks or receives some or all of its finished water from one or more wholesale waterworks. “Release” can also refer to an in-system release in which water is released from a water plant TO a service area within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks. The volume of water reported as an in-system release is usually based on a meter reading at the water plant. “Delivery” (reported by the recipient) refers to the amount of finished or raw water purchased by and delivered to a recipient waterworks FROM a wholesale waterworks. The volume of water reported as delivered is usually based on a meter reading by the receiving waterworks. “Delivery” can also refer to an in-system delivery in which water is delivered to a service area FROM a raw water source or a water plant within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks.  The volume of water reported as an in-system delivery is usually based on a meter reading at the service area connection.</li>
<li><b>Additional Withdrawal Comments</b> – Note any unusual circumstances that occurred during the reporting period. Examples may include dates of malfunctioning water meters, dates of source start up or abandonment, explanations for sharp changes in water withdrawal amounts due to temporary or permanent process shut downs or system changes, measurements reflecting clustered wells, etc.</li>
<li><b>Category (of use)</b> – write in or select the correct category of use: Agriculture, Commercial, Fossil Power, Hydro Power, Agricultural Irrigation, Manufacturing, Mining, Nuclear Power, Other, or Public Water Supply.</li>

Enclosed are the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality\'s Annual Report of Water Withdrawals forms for reporting all water withdrawals from surface water and/or groundwater sources during the period January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2022. Water withdrawal reporting is required under State Water Control Board (SWCB) Water Withdrawal Reporting Regulation (9VAC25-200). A copy of the regulation is available on this website: <u><a>https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincode/title9/agency25/chapter200/</a></u>. The purpose of withdrawal reporting is to enable appropriate planning for the State\'s future water needs through the collection of accurate information. Water Withdrawal Reporting may be submitted to DEQ either in a hardcopy format or online.

<b>Reporting Requirements:</b>

Withdrawals in Virginia for <u>ALL purposes</u> (including, but not limited to livestock production, mining operations, public water supplies, manufacturing, power production, and golf courses) must be reported by <b>anyone whose average daily withdrawal during any single month exceeds 10,000 gallons per day (300,000 gal/month)</b>. Withdrawals for agricultural <u>crop production</u> (including nurseries and sod farms) must be reported by <b>anyone whose withdrawal exceeds one million gallons in any single month</b>. The regulation requires that reporting information include the user’s name, address, sources and locations of the withdrawal, the cumulative volume of water withdrawn during each month of the calendar year, the maximum day withdrawal and the month in which it occurred, and the method of measurement used to calculate the withdrawal.

If you report groundwater withdrawals to DEQ under a groundwater withdrawal permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. You do not need to duplicate reporting for permitted groundwater withdrawals under this regulation. DEQ imports the monthly information submitted to your regional permit writer into the water withdrawal reporting database. <b>However, you must report the surface water withdrawals that also occur at permitted groundwater withdrawal sites, either as part of your permitted groundwater withdrawal reporting or through this reporting process</b>. If you report surface water withdrawals to DEQ under a Virginia Water Protection (VWP) permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. In addition to the VWP reporting requirement, you must also report under this regulation as instructed below.

<b>Online Reporting:</b>

Our goal is to make reporting your water withdrawal as easy as possible. In December of 2016, DEQ transitioned to the VAHydro web-based system that allows for online reporting of water withdrawals. If you have reported online previously, your VAHydro username is usually your email address. Please remember the VAHydro username is case-sensitive. <b>Reporting Instructions:</b>
<ul>
<li>If you did not withdraw from one or more source points (your wells, springs, or surface water intakes) in 2022, but expect you may in future years, simply input zeros on the form in the Current Withdrawals table and note “no withdrawal this year” in the "Source Status" field.</li>
<li>If the source point has been abandoned and you <u>do not</u> plan to make future withdrawals, please check the box next to "Permanently Abandoned" in the "Source Status" field, and write the year it was abandoned in the space provided.</li>  
<li>The regulation requires the use of a methodology or the installation and operation of a gaging device at or near the source point to measure the cumulative volume of groundwater and/or surface water withdrawn. The gage or methodology used must be consistent with sound generally accepted engineering practice and produce volume determinations within 10% of accuracy. Methods for estimating your withdrawal volumes are on the "Irrigation and Agriculture Water Use Estimate factsheet" located at this link: <u><a>https://www.deq.virginia.gov/water/water-quantity/water-supply-planning/water-withdrawal-reporting</a></u>. Please contact your DEQ water supply planner (contact information above) if assistance is needed in calculating your withdrawal volumes. In addition, the regulation requires retention of water withdrawal and gage calibration records by the user for a period of three years.</li>
<li>The regulation requires that you report the maximum withdrawal in a single day and the month during which the maximum day withdrawal occurred.  If you do not collect this information at this time, <u>please estimate</u> your maximum day withdrawal and note the month this withdrawal occurred. </li>
<li>As per 2020, location data is required. <b>Section § 62.1-44.38. of the Code of Virginia requires location data be provided by each user in a coordinate system (decimal degrees) for each point of withdrawal. Provide the latitude and longitude in the location section of this form.</b></li>
<li>If you have developed <u>a new source point  at an existing facility</u>, please use the form on the last page entitled “***Complete form for any additional source points.” Fill in as much information as possible. Examples of new source points include an additional pond, movement of a stream intake from one stream to a different stream, an additional groundwater well, etc.</li>
<li> If you have <u>a new source point at a new facility</u>, please contact your DEQ water supply planner.</li>
</ul>  

<b>Description of Fields on Reporting Form:</b>
<ul>
<li><b>Action Type</b> - Identifies the appropriate action – write in one of the following: Withdrawal, Delivery, or Release. “Withdrawal” refers to any withdrawal of raw water. “Release” refers to the amount of finished or raw water sold by and released from a wholesale waterworks TO a recipient waterworks. The meter reading at a wholesale waterworks is the volume of water usually reported as “released”. “Delivery” (reported by the recipient) refers to the amount of finished or raw water purchased by and delivered to a recipient waterworks FROM a wholesale waterworks. The meter reading by the receiving waterworks is the volume of water usually reported as “delivered”. </li>
<li><b>Additional Withdrawal Comments</b> – Note any unusual circumstances that occurred during the reporting period. Examples may include dates of malfunctioning water meters, dates of source start up or abandonment, explanations for sharp changes in water withdrawal amounts due to temporary or permanent process shut downs or system changes, measurements reflecting clustered wells, etc.</li>
<li><b>Category (of use)</b> – write in the correct category of use: Agriculture, Agricultural Irrigation, Commercial, Manufacturing, Mining, Fossil Power, Hydro Power, Nuclear Power, Public Water Supply, or Other.</li>
<li><b>DEQ Well ID</b> – This is a DEQ assigned identification number for groundwater wells or springs.</li> 
<li><b>Facility Feature ID, DEQ Source ID</b> – These fields are for internal DEQ use only.</li> 
<li><b>GW Permit, VDH PWSID, VPDES Permit #</b> - Enter permit numbers for applicable programs, such as the Virginia Department of Health (VDH) Public Water System Identification (PWSID) number, DEQ groundwater permit (GW PERMIT) or Industrial VPDES permit.</li> 
<li><b>Intake Location</b> – Write in the county, city, or town where the source point (withdrawal point) is located.</li> 
<li><b>Latitude and Longitude</b> – Location of source point in a coordinate system (decimal degrees).</li>
<li><b>Maximum Withdrawal in a Day, Month Max Day occurred</b> - Indicate the maximum withdrawal within a single day in million gallons (MG) and write in the month during which the maximum day occurred. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000.</li>
<li><b>Measuring Method and Source/Customer Metering</b> - Select the method used to determine water withdrawal amounts, “Metered” or “Estimated.” If your withdrawals are metered, check "Source" if the meter is at the source; check "Customer" if it is metered anywhere other than at the source. If you estimate your withdrawal amounts, please describe the method used (e.g., pump time curves, etc.).</li> 
<li><b>MPID</b> – Measuring Point Identification Number (equivalent to old VWUDS MPID).</li> 
<li><b>Source Status</b> - By default, this entry is set to "active", indicating that you did in fact withdraw water from this source point during the previous year. If there was no withdrawal or transfer of water at this source point during the previous year, but there is a plan to continue to use this source point in the future, then "No Withdrawal This Year" should be selected. If the source point or transfer is permanently abandoned, then "Permanently Abandoned" should be selected and the year the source point was removed from operation should be written in. </li>
<li><b>Source Type</b> – Write in the appropriate classification for the source point: Surface Water Intake, Groundwater Well, or Water Transfer. Transferred Water is water that is withdrawn by a waterworks owner or operator and then transferred to another waterworks owner or operator. It is water bought or sold and is not a direct withdrawal from a source.</li>
<li><b>Sub-type</b> – Write in the raw water source sub-type as a Reservoir, Stream, Well, or Spring, as appropriate.</li>
<li><b>Water Source Name</b> – Write in the commonly used name of the raw water source for the withdrawal, such as the “James River,” “Farm Pond,” or “Well #1.”</li>
<li><b>Water Withdrawal Amount or Bulk Transfers</b> - Report the withdrawal or transfer volumes in MG for JANUARY through DECEMBER. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000. Example: If your withdrawal for January was 65,323 gallons, please record as 0.065323. If your July withdrawal was 465,000 gallons, please record as 0.465. If you have any questions, contact your DEQ water supply planner.</li>
</ul>

Enclosed are the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality\'s Annual Report of Water Withdrawals forms for reporting all water withdrawals from surface water and/or groundwater sources during the period January 1, 2021 through December 31, 2021. Water withdrawal reporting is required under State Water Control Board (SWCB) Water Withdrawal Reporting Regulation (9 VAC 25-200-10), et seq. <b>Reporting Requirements:</b>

Withdrawals in Virginia for agricultural crop production (including nurseries and sod farms) must be reported by anyone whose withdrawal exceeds one million gallons in any single month.  Withdrawals in Virginia for ALL other purposes (including, but not limited to livestock production, mining operations, public water supplies, manufacturing, power production, and golf courses) must be reported by anyone whose average daily withdrawal during any single month exceeds 10,000 gallons per day.  The regulation requires that reporting information include the user’s name, address, sources and locations of the withdrawal, the cumulative volume of water withdrawn during each month of the calendar year, the maximum day withdrawal and the month in which it occurred, and the method of measurement used to calculate the withdrawal.  Water users exempted from the regulation may voluntarily report their water withdrawals.

If you report surface water withdrawals to DEQ under a Virginia Water Protection (VWP) permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. In addition to the VWP reporting requirement, you must also report under this regulation as instructed below.

<b>Description of Fields on Reporting Form:</b>
<ul>
<li><b>Action Type</b> - Identifies the appropriate action for transfers–circle one of the following: Release, Delivery. “Release” (reported by a wholesale waterworks) refers to the amount of finished or raw water sold by and released from a wholesale waterworks TO a recipient waterworks. The volume of water reported as released is usually based on a meter reading at the wholesale waterworks. A wholesale waterworks is a waterworks that treats source water as necessary to produce finished water and then delivers some or all of that finished water to another waterworks. A consecutive waterworks is a waterworks that has no water production or source facility of its own and that obtains all of its water from another permitted waterworks or receives some or all of its finished water from one or more wholesale waterworks. “Release” can also refer to an in-system release in which water is released from a water plant TO a service area within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks. The volume of water reported as an in-system release is usually based on a meter reading at the water plant. “Delivery” (reported by the recipient) refers to the amount of finished or raw water purchased by and delivered to a recipient waterworks FROM a wholesale waterworks. The volume of water reported as delivered is usually based on a meter reading by the receiving waterworks. “Delivery” can also refer to an in-system delivery in which water is delivered to a service area FROM a raw water source or a water plant within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks.  The volume of water reported as an in-system delivery is usually based on a meter reading at the service area connection.</li>
<li><b>Additional Withdrawal Comments</b> – Note any unusual circumstances that occurred during the reporting period. Examples may include dates of malfunctioning water meters, dates of source start up or abandonment, explanations for sharp changes in water withdrawal amounts due to temporary or permanent process shut downs or system changes, measurements reflecting clustered wells, etc.</li>
<li><b>Category (of use)</b> – write in or select the correct category of use: Agriculture, Commercial, Fossil Power, Hydro Power, Agricultural Irrigation, Manufacturing, Mining, Nuclear Power, Other, or Public Water Supply.</li>
<li><b>DEQ Well ID</b> – This is a DEQ assigned identification number for groundwater wells or springs.</li> 
<li><b>Facility Feature ID, DEQ Source ID</b> – These fields are for internal DEQ use only.</li>

<li><b>Intake Location</b> – Write in or select the county, city, or town where the measuring point (source withdrawal) is located.</li>

<li><b>Maximum Withdrawal in a Day, Month Max Day occurred</b> - Indicate the maximum withdrawal within a single day in million gallons (MG) and write in or select the month during which the maximum day occurred. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000.</li>
<li><b>Measuring Method and Source/Customer Metering</b> - Select the method used to determine water withdrawal amounts, “Metered” or “Estimated.” If your withdrawals are metered, check "Source" if the meter is at the source; check "Customer" if it is metered anywhere other than at the source. If you estimate your withdrawal amounts, please describe the method used (e.g., pump time curves, etc.).</li>

<li><b>Water Withdrawal Amount or Bulk Transferes</b> - Report the withdrawal or transfer volumes in MG for JANUARY through DECEMBER. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000. Example: If your withdrawal for January was 65,323 gallons, please record as 0.065323. If your July withdrawal was 465,000 gallons, please record as 0.465. If you have any questions, contact your DEQ water supply planner.</li>

Enclosed are the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality\'s Annual Report of Water Withdrawals forms for reporting all water withdrawals from surface water and/or groundwater sources during the period January 1, 2018 through December 31, 2018. Water withdrawal reporting is required under State Water Control Board (SWCB) Water Withdrawal Reporting Regulation (9 VAC 25-200-10), et seq. The purpose of withdrawal reporting is to enable appropriate planning for the State\'s future water needs through the collection of accurate information. A copy of the regulation is available on this website: <a href="url">http://www.deq.virginia.gov/Programs/Water/WaterSupplyWaterQuantity/WaterSupplyPlanning/AnnualWaterWithdrawalReporting.aspx</a> Water Withdrawal Reporting may be submitted to DEQ either in a hardcopy format or online.

<b>Reporting Requirements:</b>

Withdrawals in Virginia for agricultural crop production (including nurseries and sod farms) must be reported by anyone whose withdrawal exceeds one million gallons in any single month.  Withdrawals in Virginia for ALL other purposes (including, but not limited to livestock production, mining operations, public water supplies, manufacturing, power production, and golf courses) must be reported by anyone whose average daily withdrawal during any single month exceeds 10,000 gallons per day.  The regulation requires that reporting information include the user’s name, address, sources and locations of the withdrawal, the cumulative volume of water withdrawn during each month of the calendar year, the maximum day withdrawal and the month in which it occurred, and the method of measurement used to calculate the withdrawal.  Water users exempted from the regulation may voluntarily report their water withdrawals. 

If you report groundwater withdrawals to DEQ under a groundwater withdrawal permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements.  You do not need to duplicate reporting for permitted groundwater withdrawals under this regulation.  DEQ imports the monthly information submitted to your regional permit writer into the water withdrawal reporting database. However, you must report the surface water withdrawals that also occur at permitted groundwater withdrawal sites, either as part of your permitted groundwater withdrawal reporting or through this reporting process.  If you report surface water withdrawals to DEQ under a Virginia Water Protection (VWP) permit, please continue to do so to meet your permit requirements. In addition to the VWP reporting requirement, you must also report under this regulation as instructed below.

<b>Description of Fields on Reporting Form:</b>
<ul>
<li><b>Action Type</b> - Identifies the appropriate action for transfers–circle one of the following: Release, Delivery. “Release” (reported by a wholesale waterworks) refers to the amount of finished or raw water sold by and released from a wholesale waterworks TO a recipient waterworks. The volume of water reported as released is usually based on a meter reading at the wholesale waterworks. A wholesale waterworks is a waterworks that treats source water as necessary to produce finished water and then delivers some or all of that finished water to another waterworks. A consecutive waterworks is a waterworks that has no water production or source facility of its own and that obtains all of its water from another permitted waterworks or receives some or all of its finished water from one or more wholesale waterworks. “Release” can also refer to an in-system release in which water is released from a water plant TO a service area within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks. The volume of water reported as an in-system release is usually based on a meter reading at the water plant. “Delivery” (reported by the recipient) refers to the amount of finished or raw water purchased by and delivered to a recipient waterworks FROM a wholesale waterworks. The volume of water reported as delivered is usually based on a meter reading by the receiving waterworks. “Delivery” can also refer to an in-system delivery in which water is delivered to a service area FROM a raw water source or a water plant within a system owned and/or operated by the same waterworks.  The volume of water reported as an in-system delivery is usually based on a meter reading at the service area connection.</li>
<li><b>Additional Withdrawal Comments</b> – Note any unusual circumstances that occurred during the reporting period. Examples may include dates of malfunctioning water meters, dates of source start up or abandonment, explanations for sharp changes in water withdrawal amounts due to temporary or permanent process shut downs or system changes, measurements reflecting clustered wells, etc.</li>
<li><b>Category (of use)</b> – write in or select the correct category of use: Agriculture, Commercial, Fossil Power, Hydro Power, Agricultural Irrigation, Manufacturing, Mining, Nuclear Power, Other, or Public Water Supply.</li>
<li><b>DEQ Well ID</b> – This is a DEQ assigned identification number for groundwater wells or springs.</li> 
<li><b>Facility Feature ID, DEQ Source ID</b> – These fields are for internal DEQ use only.</li> 
<li><b>GW Permit, VDH PWSID, VPDES Permit #</b> - Enter permit numbers for applicable programs, such as the Virginia Department of Health (VDH) Public Water System Identification (PWSID) number, DEQ groundwater permit (GW PERMIT) or Industrial VPDES permit.</li> 
<li><b>Intake Location</b> – Write in or select the county, city, or town where the measuring point (source withdrawal) is located.</li> 
<li><b>Latitude and Longitude</b> – Indicate the earth coordinates of the withdrawal intake location (please enter in decimal degrees).</li>
<li><b>Maximum Withdrawal in a Day, Month Max Day occurred</b> - Indicate the maximum withdrawal within a single day in million gallons (MG) and write in or select the month during which the maximum day occurred. If your withdrawal is measured in gallons, please convert to MG by dividing by 1,000,000.</li>
<li><b>Measuring Method and Source/Customer Metering</b> - Select the method used to determine water withdrawal amounts, “Metered” or “Estimated.” If your withdrawals are metered, check "Source" if the meter is at the source; check "Customer" if it is metered anywhere other than at the source. If you estimate your withdrawal amounts, please describe the method used (e.g., pump time curves, etc.).</li> 
<li><b>Measuring Point Feature ID</b> – Measuring Point Identification Number (equivalent to old VWUDS MPID).</li>
